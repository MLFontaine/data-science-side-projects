Kevin:

I'm from Minnesota, and my roommate is from Indiana, and both of us think our state is the most Midwestern state. Can you give us a list of most Midwestern states, ranked?

I can, although I'm biased, because I'm from Minnesota, and I have hard time thinking of a whiter, more passive-aggressive variant of humanity than your typical Minnesotan. Here is my list. Please note that I have included the Plains States here, even though I think the Plains States are their own distinct brand of freakshow.

1. Wisconsin. They have the girth, the fried foodstuffs, and the accent. Plus they aren't as stuffy as Minnesotans are. Minnesota is famous for lakes, and Wisconsin is famous for cheese, and cheese is a way more Midwestern thing than lakes.

2. Minnesota. My home state is the worst . Also, the most famous movie set in Minnesota isn't even named after Minnesota, which is weird.

3. Iowa. It's all made of corn, right? Even the people are made of corn.

4. Indiana . "We're just simple folk round here (roots for the white guy on every basketball team)."

5. North Dakota. Of the two Dakotas, North Dakota is way more Midwestern. South Dakota has the Badlands and old gold mines and all kinds of cool Western frontier shit; North Dakota just has Tom Brokaw speaking to you through the clouds as you drive through it.

6. Nebraska. I grew up with the old Big Ten, so I still don't really accept Nebraska as a real part of the Midwest, even though the people here are stoic enough and boring enough to qualify. "'Round here, we don't tell our kids we love them. That's for showy folk."

7. Michigan. Detroit kinda cancels out the more Midwestern parts of the state, which is too bad, because I maintain that "the Michigan" is underrated as far as horrible Midwestern accents go.

8. Illinois. Chicago makes Illinois way cooler than any other Midwest state. We should airlift Chicago and put it down somewhere better than a fucking arctic wind farm. If you took Chicago away and left just the "AW JEEZ GUYS" parts of the state where Leitch is from, Illinois would rank way higher.

9. Kansas. Not the Midwest. It's just crazy, Oklahoma-type people.

10. South Dakota. See North Dakota.

11. Missouri. Also not the Midwest. It's the South now. They've shot enough unarmed minorities to qualify .

12. Ohio. Like Pennsylvania, Ohio is its own batshit-crazy thing. I don't even think of Ohio as being American. I don't want to get any Ohio on me.

To me, Midwestern people are white and boring and have awful taste in food. Hence, Wisconsin and Minnesota.

James:

What's the best way to get out of jury duty? At first, I thought it was just plain pretending to be racist, but I'm going to college full-time, and that seems to be the best excuse so far, since they can't make you work around your schedule.

I have weaseled out of jury duty twice in my life (once was grand jury duty, which you REALLY do not want), and I can tell you that judges could give half a fuck if your jury service interferes with your work or class schedules. They have heard every single excuse from every single asshole, and it pleases them to inconvenience your selfish ass. If I were a judge, I'd force only the busiest people to serve. The only way you get out of jury duty is through sheer luck. The numbers are usually on your side, so you just have to sit there and pray that they don't pick you, or that they do pick you and then find you naturally repellent enough to dismiss you.

Because most of the time, people don't have the nerve to declare themselves racist in front of a judge. Not only are you lying, but you will shockingly not want people to think you are racist! And you will feel like a terrible American (because you are). The last time I got out of jury duty, I went to the Maryland courthouse and they showed a taped video from a famous lawyer on TV (I can't remember who it was, possibly Sam Waterston) who talked about our patriotic duty to serve on a jury of your peers. And as cynical as I am, I watched that video and felt like shit for wanting to get out of there. I did a full 180. I was ready to serve, man.

Then they brought us into the courtroom for voir dire and I changed my mind immediately. They told us the trial would last WEEKS because it involved a man accused of sexually assaulting his neighbor's children, which OH HOLY SHIT THE WORLD IS HORRIBLE. A clerk got up and asked the jury pool a series of questions, such as, "Do you know the defendant?" and "Are you not a citizen of the United States?" and "Are you armed right now?" (At one point, they asked if any of the jury pool members knew each other, and one dude looked at me and raised his hand and the clerk asked how we knew each other, and I said, "I write for a few places, and I think he reads some of my stuff," but that wasn't true AT ALL. This guy's kid went to school with my kid, and I hadn't recognized him. "Oh, you write?" he asked me. I've never felt like such a dipshit. I should be shot into fucking space.)

Anytime people raised their hand to one of the clerk's questions, they would get called up to the bench to talk to the judge, with the attorneys standing nearby. And they turned on a white noise machine so that the rest of the jury pool couldn't hear the conversation. It was the weirdest shit ever. I tried to read lips, but it was no use. If you really want to serve on a jury, you never raise your hand for any of these questions. At one point, I did raise my hand and was called up in order to explain my past encounter with a child molester (which was a true story ). I also snuck in that I was a busy man and had many children of my own to tend to, and that having children made me not like child molesters. The judge gave NO fucks, at all.

I sat back down, and then they began to formally seat the jury. I was called into the box. I was gonna be juror #1. There is a formal language to court proceedings that I do not understand, so when the prosecutor approved of my presence, she said something like "The juror is obsequiated." It all happened very fast. I was confused. And then the defense lawyer traded whispers with his client (who TOTALLY looked guilty) and said he formally protested my selection. Then they kicked me out of the box, and I was done for the day. I don't have to serve again for 10 years.

The defense never tells you why they object to your presence. I'd like to think they were scared off by my good looks and Jeterian moral rectitude. They could not handle my truth. I would have fried that bastard. Anyway, don't bother trying to get out of jury duty. If they want you, they will claim you.

How do we know that this Bill Simmons/ESPN rift isn't a page from WWE? Think about it: Say Simmons wanted to go on vacation for a few weeks. The storyline pops up: ESPN is the evil corporation stroking the ego that is the NFL. The common folk are speaking up, but don't have a hero. In steps Bill! Bill breaks against his employer, stands for the common man, gains tons of new followers/readers, and then he gets "banned" for three weeks.

I think there's some of that to his suspension. It was a "real" suspension , but he obviously knew that daring his bosses to discipline him would result in his being disciplined, because ESPN is the sort of place where talking out of turn is regarded as crass and unprofessional. WE SIMPLY DON'T DO THOSE THINGS HERE AT ESPN. They always like to consider themselves above the fray. Meanwhile, Stephen A. Smith is making fart noises at Skip Bayless from inside a gray box.

Anyway, Leitch put it best when he said Simmons likes to be with ESPN but not considered of ESPN. He likes all the money and resources and exposure that ESPN provides, but he doesn't want to be seen as one of the loser drones working there. He wants to be PUNK, man. That's why he got his own boutique site with writers he can collect and put in his little trophy case, and he gets to pretend that the site doesn't benefit from having the ESPN-traffic fire hose attached to it. The fact that Simmons operates next to old-school hot-take morons like Wilbon and Cowherd makes his shit seem twice as fresh.

So what better time to get suspended than right before the beginning of the NBA season? Simmons has a new Grantland TV show coming out next month (!) that doesn't appear to be in any jeopardy of being scuttled, and he already told Richard "Thirsty" Deitsch that doing TV exhausted him and his family. It's the perfect time for him to rest up, and for ESPN to look like it's being all proactive with loose cannons and shit. You can make a "real" decision to suspend a guy while still being aware of the convenience for all parties involved. And having Simmons around benefits ESPN because he is their model sports fan. ESPN is structured to cultivate know-it-all sports fans, and there is no bigger sports know-it-all than that guy. They want EVERY fan to be a Simmons.

Andy:

Assuming a person were to voluntarily have his/her foot run over by a car or truck just to see if they could do it without getting injured while attempting this (said person is standing straight up with one foot out farther than the other for easy access), what would be the best speed for the vehicle to drive to minimize the chance of injury? I'm of the opinion that if you just creep over the foot, it would be more agonizing, but if you gunned it over the foot, the impact alone might turn the bones in your foot to dust. Any ideas?

Patrick:

Recently I was with my wife and a group of close friends while a news story recapped the night O.J. Simpson ran. We began discussing where we were and what we were doing that night and how weird it was. Because of what happened to me that night, I could clearly recall where I was. I told them I was at a friend's parents place that had a big house on one side of the huge property and a "barn" on the other side. The barn was more of a party place for derby parties and such, as half of it was a furnished apartment with wet bar and big screen etc. That night my buddy and I planned on watching the NBA Finals and enjoying some shrooms from the cow pasture next door. With recent rain, hallucinogenic mushrooms were plentiful if you didn't mind picking them out of cow shit.

When the television coverage switched to O.J. on the run, we honestly thought we were hallucinating and kept asking each other if what we were watching was real. We drank and watched in utter amazement and laughing hysterically at how absurd it was. Later, after exhausting the alcohol supply, my buddy asked if I would drive across the property to the main house and get a 12-pack from the garage fridge. It was pouring rain, and I questioned if my Honda Civic would make it down the dirt road to the house, and he assured me it would, but told me to drive back in his Mom's new Jeep 4x4. A couple weeks before, a horse his mom owned since she was a teenager (nearly 30 years; I never knew horses lived that long) had died, and she was so grief-stricken that his dad bought the Jeep she had always wanted to cheer her up.

On my way back to the barn with the beer, I decided to take a short cut, which went fine until I started to get stuck, and the front wheels of the Jeep began to sink and bog down. The more I tried to get out, the more the front of the Jeep sank. After churning up the mud for a couple minutes, I realized I wasn't getting loose and would have to walk back to the barn and get my friend and their tractor to pull it out of the stinking mud. And when I say stinking, I am not kidding: It literally smelled awful. I got back to the barn and told him what I had done, and he laughed and said we would pull it out the next morning because he was tripping too hard to function. After that I blacked out.

I woke up the next morning on the barn sofa with his brother standing over me yelling at the top of his lungs. His mom was outside crying and losing her mind screaming at her son. Apparently I hadn't just gotten the Jeep stuck in some random spot, I had gotten it stuck in the grave of her horse and then sort of dug it up with the wheels. That was a long morning.

The longest.
